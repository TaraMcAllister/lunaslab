Fun Facts

Uranium is the main ingredient in nuclear energy and weapons.

The only nuclear bomb ever used contained Uranium. It was dropped on August 6th 1945. It was 3 metres long and was dropped on the Japanese city of Hiroshima, destroying the city and all its inhabitant in the blink of an eye.

Uranium is radioactive which means it decays rapidly and gives off harmful rays.

Marie Curie discovered radioactivity. She eventually died from cancer from being exposed to too much radioactive material. She did not know the rays were harmful.

We can also use Uranium to make electricity - Homer's workplace in The Simpsons? Nuclear energy plant!
